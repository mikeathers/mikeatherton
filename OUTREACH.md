# Outreach

Use private, direct, low-volume outreach first.

## Priority channels

1. Warm intros
2. Direct founder / operator email
3. LinkedIn DMs
4. Upwork with premium positioning

## Who to contact first

- founders you already know
- ex-colleagues now in startups
- investors with messy portfolio companies
- heads of engineering / product in small AI-native teams
- operators with clear platform or workflow pain

## First message shape

Keep it short:

1. who you help
2. what kind of problem you fix
3. why you are reaching out
4. one light next step

## Warm intro message

```text
I’m doing some part-time work helping small teams tighten engineering quality, technical decision-making, and the ugly workflows that start breaking once AI makes output faster but messier.

If you know a founder or engineering lead dealing with that kind of problem, I’d appreciate an intro.
```

## Direct founder message

```text
Hi {Name},

I help small teams tighten engineering quality, decision-making, and messy technical workflows when AI has made output faster but harder to judge.

That usually means things like weak review discipline, architecture drift, unclear ownership, broken internal processes, or platform work that keeps stalling.

If that sounds relevant, happy to compare notes and see if a short audit or fractional support would be useful.

Mike
```

## Upwork positioning

Do not position as:
- full-stack developer
- general freelancer

Position as:
- fractional technical lead
- AI engineering quality / guardrails
- technical workflow and platform rescue

## What to look for on Upwork

- AI startup cleanup
- CTO support
- technical lead / architecture review
- engineering process improvement
- internal tool rescue
- platform stabilization
- delivery / code quality issues
